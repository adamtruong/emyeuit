A DAY IN THE LIFE OF A SOFTWARE ENGINEER

(https://youtu.be/vTXzJVFelao)

Name: Kenny Gunderman
Place: the Midwest, United States
Work at: Two Men And A Truck

  • Wake up
  • Eat breakfast
  • Go to work.
  • Online meeting to discuss work
  • Start coding
  • Hard, go to Stackoverflow for an answer.
  • Stand up, walk around, get a snack.
  • Get lunch
  • Then continuous coding.
  • Until the end of the shift
  • Go to the gym
  • Get back home to rest.

    So, most of the day, he spent time working and doing everything else in the company. Most people he meets in a day are through the screen of his laptop because they work from home or another state. The challenge of this job is that your teammate may be in a different place or country so that it is pretty challenging to meet and discuss face-to-face. Therefore, decision-making and solving problems may not be fast and efficient.

    • (https://youtu.be/j1fc0FlCjyI)

    Name: Justin Hammond
    Place: Lehi, Utah
    Work at: Silicon Slopes

  • Wake up and check for the baby
  • Get a good breakfast.
  • Prep for work
  • Check GitHub to see if someone has opened an issue and then respond to them.
  • Check email to see if any team member has pushed code that needs review.
  • Code review time: Check if the coworker has submitted and left feedback. This is important because if the code has a bug and you use that code, it can cause damage
  • Check feedback for my code, then deal with the problem. They noticed me and resubmitted the code again
  • Check if there are any jobs still available to do on the board
  • Start with a simple task
  • Done with family matters
  • Take lunch with his baby
  • Go back to work
  • Online meeting to discuss what each person did and plans to and any issues that arise while performing tasks
  • Back to coding: building an internal tool that will help us facilitate working faster as a team. Someday make customer-facing features
  • Work on various categories: features, bug fixes, scripts, databases, internal tools, documentation
  • Take a short break
  • More meetings
  • Pair programming: team up with another member to watch and learn. This is very important, so he tried to do this as often as possible.
  • Standing up after sitting too long and finishing the rest of the work by checking emails, slack messages, submitting code reviews and closing all finished tasks
  • Double-check again
  • Finish work. After that, eat dinner with the family, watch shows, and walk around the house

    He spends most of the time inside his house because he works from home to look after the baby. In the morning, he doesn’t do much coding. Most of the time is spent checking emails, coworker codes, and addressing the problems with his code that his coworker has pointed out. It's mostly on GitHub and emails. When all of the issues have been dealt with, he will check for any tasks that he can do on the board anstart with something simple. Then, come noon, he goes out to take the baby to the doctor for a checkup and follows that by having lunch. When the lunch is finished, he gets back to work and starts by having a meeting with his coworkers to discuss what they did and the plan for future tasks. After the meeting, he returns to coding for an internal tool the team will use. His job can involve various categories: features, bug fixes, scripts, databases, internal tools, documentation. He takes a short break to prep for the next meet when the device is done. After that, he pairs with a coworker to see each other code and learn about freaceach person’s experience the day; he will finish the rest of the tasks while standing for an extended time. Double-check all of his emails and messages, then close all open tasks. For the evening, he eats dinner with his family and watches shows. The challenge of this job is that you need to have various knowledge about computers, such as link websites, bug fixes, documental, and coding, to do the work smoothly and be in charge of projects with others. Also, having to sit before the monitor for 8-9 hours straight, it is predicted that health problems, including eye strain and backache, will be challenging for a Software Engineer like Justin. This is why working out or going jogging in the morning has become a new trend among IT Engineers to keep fit and healthy.

    • DAY IN THE LIFE OF A JAPANESE GAME PROGRAMMER

    (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e_TxH59MclA)

    Name: Masa
    Place: Tokyo, Japan
    Work at: Bandai Namco Studio

  • Wake up at 8:15 am
  • Personal hygiene
  • Dress up (need 5 to 10 minutes)
  • Go to work at around 9:10 am by train
  • Before he starts to work, he continuously checks his email and the internal company’s social network and has a quick breakfast.
  • Start coding about game
  • At noon, he has lunch with his colleagues
  • He comes back to work at 1:00 pm
  • He finishes up his tasks at 6:00 pm

    He always wakes up between 8 am to 8:20 am and then does personal hygiene. He goes to work by underground train, which is typical in Japan. The first thing he does when coming to his company is to check his email and the internal company’s social network and has a quick breakfast with a juice box and a calorie bar. He spends most of his time coding, sharing and discussing the upcoming game project with his colleagues. At noon he has lunch and comes back to work at 1 pm. He finishes his work at 6 pm, and then he hangs out and has dinner with his friends. Coming up with new game ideas is the biggest challenge for game programmers. Gamers are always eager to experience something out of this world, so game programmers have to spend a lot of time brainstorming new features constantly. Just one repetitive factor will flop the game, so game programmers might also be a bit stressed.

    • DAY IN THE LIFE OF A GOOGLE SOFTWARE ENGINEER

    (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a0glBQXOcl4)

    Name: Neil Fraser
    Place: Mountain View, California, US
    Works at: Google Headquarters, currently at home

  • Checks GitHub pull requests
  • Reviews his coworkers’ codes
  • Push his codes for his coworkers to review
  • Debugging scripts and programs
  • Reviewing security issues
  • Coding the main tasks at the office
  • Interviewing new candidates for Google
  • Working on his other personal open-source projects
  • Looking after his daughter

    He goes to work at 7:30 am on Netflix's free shuttle bus. To save time, he starts coding on the bus until he arrives at the office. Then he will have breakfast at the canteen before going to his seat. At 9 am, he continues his work and takes a break at 10:30. He will grab some lunch at 12:30 with his colleagues and go for a walk to digest the food. At 1:30, he will have a meeting with co-workers to discuss the new project and new ideas for upcoming events. He goes back to work at 3 pm and finished at 4 pm. He takes the shuttle bus to go home and still work on the bus. In my opinion, the challenge of this job is that people need to work at the head office no matter what. As you can see, he needs to travel from San Francisco to Los Gatos to work, which take him around more than 1 hour. That’s why he needs to work on the bus to save time and reduce his workload of a day. Moreover, with the distance in terrain and having to take the bus early, he doesn’t have time to do exercise, which can affect his health.
