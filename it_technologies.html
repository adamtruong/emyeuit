BLOCKCHAIN AND CRYPTOCURRENCIES

Blockchain and cryptocurrencies are currently the two terms people seek the most in 2021. Let's discover more about these technologies and their functions. Usually, most people confuse Blockchain and cryptocurrencies, thinking that these two are the same. Although the two are different initiatives, both are closely linked. It is worth noticing that the uses of Blockchain can expand to other areas beyond its features that brings cryptocurrencies to life.

A cryptocurrency act like real currency, this type of currency can be traded online and converted to other currencies. Many companies have released their kind of cryptocurrency, which can explicitly trade for the good or service that the company provides. The rise of cryptocurrencies is based on the independence of the currency as no organization or government can control certain currencies like Bitcoin, Etherium and more because these currencies run independently. Moreover, the demands of the community value the price of these currencies, so over time, cryptocurrencies prevent inflation and increase their value for investors. And decentralized processing and recording system with every transaction through cryptocurrencies can be more secure than traditional payment systems. The operation behind cryptocurrencies to work and maintain security is Blockchain, which is the foundation of this technology.

A blockchain is a distributed database shared among the nodes of a computer network. As a database, a blockchain stores information electronically in digital form. Furthermore, Blockchain is designed to record all transactions on a P2P network. Blockchain acts as an anonymous ledger. Although the details of transactions are kept secret, the catalogue is public. So people can view it to confirm that a transaction has taken place or not. Blockchain technology helps to reduce costs, increase transparency and bring efficiency. Satoshi Nakamoto created the Blockchain to record transactions made with Bitcoin, thus becoming a public ledger of transactions. His breakthrough has made Bitcoin the first cryptocurrency to use Blockchain in recording all transactions without the intervention of a third party or central authority. Most of the time, the operation of cryptocurrencies is based on Blockchain. To mine cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Blockchain is necessary and adequate. The Blockchain contains a set of "blocks", where each block includes details about transaction data or transactions made with the cryptocurrency, and when stacking those blocks on each other, it will create a chain. Each block comprises puzzles that miners can solve to validate transactions. After successfully solving the problem, a new block is developed and published to other nodes. Miners are then rewarded for completing the process. The reason why cryptocurrencies are more secure than traditional payment is based on how Blockchain works. If someone tries to remove or replace a block from the bottom of the chain, that action will destroy the entire structure because each block in the chain has the same information from the previous block encoded in it. Replacing a block with other information (different cryptography) will not match the last block and disconnect all blocks that follow it. Therefore, the information in this chain will be considered untrue. That is why blockchain technology can be viewed as a breakthrough in security.

    ROBOTS

    Robots have been no stranger to humans all over the years. These intelligent automation machines are exposed as our lives develop and upgrade over time. Nowadays, with the development of technology, robots have appeared in most countries globally, which can make human life more convenient, easier, and more efficient.

    A robot is a machine controlled by the computer or the electronic circuit board, automatically performing complex actions without human involvement. The robot was made by humans' intelligence, creativity, and determination, characterised by virtual and artificial. Generally, there are five types of robots, which is:

  • Pre-programmed robots (example: a mechanical arm on an automotive assembly line),
  • Humanoid robots (example: Hanson Robotics’ Sophia),
  • Autonomous robots (example: the Roomba vacuum cleaner),
  • Teleoperated robots (example: the PUMA 560),
  • Augmenting robots (example: robotic prosthetic limbs).

    • Pre-programmed robots are machines that only do the tasks people tell them, and they cannot change their duty by themselves during the process. Humanoid robots resemble the human appearance, which performs human-like activities. Autonomous robots are auto robots or autobot that perform tasks without human control. Teleoperated robots, or telerobotics, are remotely controlled robots that operators can command from afar or safe distance. Augmenting robots are designed to supplement or replace a person's existing talents; they can be used in medical surgeries and military operations.

    People have had to go through centuries to improve, develop, and upgrade robots to reach today's achievements. Like a famous quote say “Big things have small beginnings”, the word "robot" comes from the Czech term "robota", which means "forced labour", firstborn in the play RUR (Rossumovi Univerzální Roboti-Rossum's space robots) by the writer K. Čapek in 1922. However, until 1942, the term robotic became popular and more widespread in society thanks to the American author Isaac Asimov with his hugely successful stories about robots. Asimov envisioned a future in which robots obey Asimov's three laws of robotics – robots shall not harm people; robots shall obey people, and robots shall avoid activities that could hurt themselves. In 1954, the first programmable robot arm was designed by two American engineers George Devol and Joseph Engleberger, creating the world’s first industrial robot. And in the next two years, these two intelligence engineers had formed the world's first robot company: Unimation, Inc. In 1963, the first computer-controlled robotic arm was built to aid the disabled at Rancho Los Amigos Hospital in Downey, California. In 1973, the T3, (The Tomorrow Tool), the first commercially available minicomputer-controlled industrial robot, was released by Cincinnati Milacron Corporation. In 2000, the robotic industry achieved a lot of success, such as:

  • Honda successfully produced ASIMO Humanoid Robot,
  • Sony released the humanoid Sony Dream Robot,
  • The da Vinci Surgery System is the first robotic surgery system authorised by the Food and Drug Administration.
  • There are around 742 500 mechanical industries in the world.

The robot reached the next level when in October 2017, Sophia – an AI robot became the first robot to receive citizenship, which was Saudi Arabian citizenship, and became the first-ever non-human and first-ever Innovation Champion of the United Nations Development Programme right in the next month.

    • I. Notable technologies of modern cybersecurity


    Through decades, cybersecurity technology has continuously been evolving to protect the users’ privacy and safety and the cyber world in general. In this part, I will be discussing the characteristics and the impact, both present and future, of some of the most recent cybersecurity technologies.
    Firstly, it is the Two-Factor Authentication. Ever since Deep Learning and Big Data was invented, the cybersecurity of the modern world has been utilising them almost all the time, mostly seen in well-known companies such as Meta, Google, or Amazon. From what I have seen how Two-Factor Authentication works, all users’ data will be stored in massive storage. When authorizing access to something, users will be prompted to input some parameters such as Date of Birth, recent comments, or a One-Time Passcode. After that, the parameters will be compared to that user’s true data in the storage. If all of them are correct, the user is free to access them [1]. Because of its simplicity and high security, numerous companies have been making use of it, notable examples are Facebook, Google, YouTube. Personally, Two-Factor Authentication will likely remain in use in the next 5 or 6 years. But on the dark side of cybersecurity, hackers have also been coming up with more dangerous attacks, which means that Two-Factor Authentication methods have to step up the game.
    Speaking of Big Data, data mining has also been a valuable tool to track users and clients’ behaviours on the servers. To put it more specifically, it is called Behavioral Analytics. Behavioural Analytics can help cybersecurity officers trace potential security threats through abnormal data transmissions or transactions, therefore efficiently hunting down the threats. We have also seen many data mining applications on digital marketing and advertising, especially Facebook or Google. For example, Facebook will “mine” each user’s search requests or interactions and recommends ads/posts/groups related to those. Though its accuracy is still questionable sometimes, in my opinion, data mining will nonetheless be useful in other subjects such as medical treatment or online shopping apps in the near future, by using the same principles, e.g. recommending products based on user’s interactions, updating unusual health data from a patient,…
    The 3rd technology that I happen to find ambitiously intriguing is the Intel vPro authentication chips. As far as I can tell, it has quite a few interesting traits that might replace all current means of authentication in the near future. Basically, the chip will be embedded in the device (mostly computers or servers), and after installing it, the user will have to install the Intel Authentication application on their smartphones. The application will tap into the phones’ Bluetooth signal to transmit and compare the user’s password on the attached device. If everything matches, the device will automatically grant access without having to type the password in[2]. This is extremely useful if you set a really cryptic password and forgot it, especially if you are a server admin when you have to deal with a lot of long passwords to avoid brute force attacks. Additionally, it can also counter keyloggers, since keyloggers only track keystrokes on the keyboard, which means that if the user does not type, they will be rendered useless. Not to mention, the Intel vPro chips also have multi-level authentication, which means that it can take multiple parameters to authorize, let it be fingerprints, face ID, passwords, PINs, etc. Speaking of PINs, the PIN layout of the Intel Authentication app can also swap every now and then, and only requires the user to tap on the screen[3]. These add up more layers of security to the system, and also furthermore disabling keyloggers. Even though these vPro chips have been released for more than 5 years, they are still only used in servers or mainframes. But in the future, with more updates, I am persuaded that these vPro chips will show their full capability when Intel releases them to all users around the world.
    Through these examples, we can see that common technologies of the modern world have opened a large number of opportunities for the cyberworld. These upgrades may sound self-explanatory and sometimes might be underestimated as easy-to-come-up. But the core problem here is that it is nearly impossible to build because of hardware limitations, or to put it another way, if Big Data, Deep Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Bluetooth, Machine Learning,…did not exist, these cybersecurity technologies would not exist either.

    II. Their worldwide impacts


    Regarding the Intel vPro chips and the Intel Authentication app, it is speculated that they will completely change the way users log in to their computers. To put it more specifically, they will replace any common means of authorization such as passwords. Additionally, these changes will mainly focus on server admins and employees in tech companies, as the vPro chips are aiming for modern enterprises or their mainframes for testing as far as I can tell. With Intel Authentication, employees and admins, again as I have said above, will log in to their devices by Bluetooth signals, instead of typing in passwords. Though it is still unknown that whether or not they will make an impact on most computer users. Nevertheless, vPro chips are guaranteed to provide a simpler and more secured authorization method to all users.
    The Two-Factor Authentication technology has greatly impacted website authentication, especially on wide-area domains that monitor millions of users. It adds another dense layer of security, making it harder for hackers to sneak in or brute-forcing through. As most huge domains nowadays like Google, YouTube, or Facebook have been utilizing this technology, we can logically argue that most users around the world have been impacted by Two-Factor Authentication. In particular, when we sign in (for example, YouTube), a One-Time Passcode is usually sent to our phone or email account to verify that it is absolutely you signing in. This prevents account breaches, even if the hacker has the username and password since they have no way of accessing that OTP. However, since Two-Factor Authentication is still optional on most websites, some users often skip it, which then leads to their account being exposed to threats. That means, our most major challenge is to spread the importance of Two-Factor Authentication. Only when it is overcome, everyone will be safe.
    Lastly, it is Behavioral Analytics. It is predicted that Behavioral Analytics will be a must for all entrepreneurs to market and advertise their products, services, or their companies themselves. Additionally, obsolete ways of advertising, even though they will not be replaced, will see a significant decrease, as most entrepreneurs will shift to digital marketing, rather than putting signs on the road, renting someone for advertising, etc. Behavioural Analytics will not replace nor make any redundancies. It will just add more simplicity, effectiveness, and efficiency to advertisements and services.

    III. Personal impacts


    From my experiences, Two-Factor Authentication is undoubtedly a must-have for all of my accounts, whether it is Facebook, Google, Outlook, etc. Back then, I was just like every non-technophilea who does not care that much about security and skipped it, and the outcome is that I lost 2 Gmail accounts. But now, I have learned it the hard way, and after using Two-Factor Authentication for a few years, I feel like I am in total protection and security and have never lost any accounts since then. It might be tedious sometimes that I have to do some extra steps to gain access to my account, unlike before when I would just punch in the username and password. But now, I do not want to lose my data anymore, so I decided to proceed with it.
    Regarding the vPro chips, I have to admit that I am a PC gamer myself, so I would prefer high-end CPUs like Intel core i7 or i9, and since both of them are not vPro chips, there might be even less chance for me to even try out one of those. I am looking forward to seeing Intel integrating the vPro to the Skylake family (since i7 and i9 are all in it), but it is going to be in the far future. Still, if I were to try one of them now with Intel Authentication, it might be a bit awkward at first, since I have to raise my phone instead of typing in the password to unlock the computer. But overall, I think it will not affect my computer experience that much, or might even remain the same as usual. As for my parents, I think it is going to discourage them from using computers even more because they are so used to the traditional way. The same would apply to my friends unless they are computer geeks like me.
    Lastly, about Behavioral Analytics, it depends on what app I am using, to say the least. About the Facebook ads, they are relatively bad in my view. They do not seem to match any of the topics I want or search, and sometimes they are flat out adware or malware. However, YouTube recommendations work smoothly for me. It recommends just the right topic, and as a matter of fact, I learned a lot of things related to computers and networking through these recommendations. I even got to know a lot of quality computer-related channels like Computerphile, Numberphile, freeCodeCamp, etc. Overall, it is most useful for me, but I am not sure if it is for my relatives and friends. They do not seem to care much about it, but if they do, I am guessing that they might feel the same as me.
